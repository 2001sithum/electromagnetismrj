Designing an LC Circuit

Question (Jan/Feb 2023, Q2a.iii): Design an LC circuit with \( V_m = 100 \) V, \( I_m = 10 \) A, and \( f = 1000 \) Hz. Find L and C.

Solution

1. Energy Conservation: \( \frac{1}{2} C V_m^2 = \frac{1}{2} L I_m^2 \implies L/C = (V_m/I_m)^2 = 100 \implies L = 100C \).
2. Resonance Formula: \( \omega_0 = 2\pi f = 1/\sqrt{LC} \).
3. Solve for C: Substitute L=100C into the frequency equation: \( (2000\pi)^2 = 1/(100C^2) \). \( C = 1/(10 \times 2000\pi) \approx 15.9 \text{ µF} \).
4. Solve for L: \( L = 100C \approx 1.59 \text{ mH} \).

Quality Factor (Q) and Voltage Magnification

Question (Jan/Feb 2023, Q2b): (i) What is meant by Q-factor? (ii) Prove voltage magnification = Q at resonance.

Solution

(i) Q-Factor: It is a dimensionless parameter describing the sharpness of resonance. High Q means a narrow bandwidth and high selectivity. It is defined as \( Q = \omega_0 L / R \).
(ii) Proof of Voltage Magnification: At resonance, \( I_m = V_{s,m} / R \). The peak voltage across the inductor is \( V_{L,m} = I_m X_L = (V_{s,m}/R)(\omega_0 L) \).
$$ V_{L,m} = V_{s,m} \left( \frac{\omega_0 L}{R} \right) = Q \cdot V_{s,m} $$
Thus, the voltage across the inductor is magnified by a factor of Q compared to the source voltage.

Capacitor Behavior with Changing Frequency

Question (Nov/Dec 2023, Q2b.ii): A capacitor has \(I_m=3\)A with a \(V_m=110\)V source. Find the new \(I_m\) and \(X_C\) if frequency is doubled.

Solution

Initial Reactance: \( X_{C1} = V_m/I_{m1} = 110/3 \approx 36.67 \, \Omega \).
New Reactance: Since \( X_C \propto 1/f \), doubling f halves \(X_C\). \( X_{C2} = X_{C1} / 2 \approx 18.33 \, \Omega \).
New Peak Current: \( I_{m2} = V_m/X_{C2} = 110 / 18.33 = 6 \text{ A} \).

Average Power in an RLC Circuit

Question (Nov/Dec 2023, Q2a.ii): Show derivations for average power, peak current, and phase angle.

Innovative Derivation Path

1. Impedance in terms of \( \omega_0 \): Start with \( Z^2 = R^2 + (\omega L - 1/(\omega C))^2 \). Substitute \( C = 1/(\omega_0^2 L) \) to get \( Z^2 = R^2 + \frac{L^2(\omega^2 - \omega_0^2)^2}{\omega^2} \).
2. Average Power: Start with \( P_{av} = I_{rms}^2 R = V_{rms}^2 R / Z^2 \). Substitute \(Z^2\) from above and multiply the numerator and denominator by \( \omega^2 \) to get the desired form:
$$ P_{av} = \frac{V_{rms}^2 R \omega^2}{R^2 \omega^2 + L^2(\omega^2 - \omega_0^2)^2} $$
3. Peak Current: \( I_m = V_m/Z \). Using the expression for Z from Step 1 gives:
$$ I_m = \frac{V_m \omega}{\sqrt{R^2\omega^2 + L^2(\omega^2 - \omega_0^2)^2}} $$
4. Phase Angle: \( \tan\phi = (X_L - X_C)/R \). Factoring out L and substituting for C gives:
$$ \tan\phi = \frac{L(\omega^2 - \omega_0^2)}{R\omega} $$

Future Exam Preparation: Model Questions

Question 1: A series RLC circuit (R=25 \(\Omega\), L=60 mH, C=10 µF) is connected to a 120V (RMS), 60 Hz source. Calculate: (a) the impedance Z, (b) the RMS current \(I_{rms}\), (c) the power factor, and (d) the average power dissipated.

Model Answer 1

1. Calculate Reactances: \( \omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi(60) \approx 377 \) rad/s.
\( X_L = \omega L = 377 \times 0.060 \approx 22.62 \, \Omega \).
\( X_C = 1/(\omega C) = 1/(377 \times 10 \times 10^{-6}) \approx 265.25 \, \Omega \).

2. Calculate Impedance (Z): \( Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} = \sqrt{25^2 + (22.62 - 265.25)^2} \approx 243.8 \, \Omega \).

3. Calculate RMS Current: \( I_{rms} = V_{rms}/Z = 120 / 243.8 \approx 0.492 \text{ A} \).

4. Calculate Power Factor: \( \cos\phi = R/Z = 25 / 243.8 \approx 0.103 \). Since \( X_C > X_L \), the circuit is capacitive and the power factor is leading.

5. Calculate Average Power: \( P_{av} = V_{rms} I_{rms} \cos\phi = (120)(0.492)(0.103) \approx 6.08 \text{ W} \). (Check: \( P_{av} = I_{rms}^2 R = (0.492)^2 \times 25 \approx 6.05 \text{ W} \). The small difference is due to rounding.)
