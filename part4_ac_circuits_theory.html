Part 4: AC Circuit Theory & Resonance

Reactance and Impedance

Short Notes: Reactance

Reactance (X) is the opposition to AC current from inductors and capacitors. Inductive reactance \(X_L = \omega L\) increases with frequency. Capacitive reactance \(X_C = 1/(\omega C)\) decreases with frequency.

Question (Jan/Feb 2023, Q2a.i): Define reactance in terms of rms and maximum values.

Solution

Reactance is the ratio of voltage to current for a reactive component. Since \( V_{rms} = V_m/\sqrt{2} \) and \( I_{rms} = I_m/\sqrt{2} \), the ratio is the same for both.
$$ X_L = \frac{V_{L,rms}}{I_{rms}} = \frac{V_{L,m}}{I_m} \quad \text{and} \quad X_C = \frac{V_{C,rms}}{I_{rms}} = \frac{V_{C,m}}{I_m} $$

Kirchhoff's Voltage Law in RLC Circuits

Question (Jan/Feb 2023, Q2a.ii & Nov/Dec 2023, Q2b.iii): The sum of peak voltages across R, L, and C often exceeds the source peak voltage. Explain why this does not violate KVL.

Explanation

This does not violate KVL because KVL applies to instantaneous voltages. The peak voltages across R, L, and C occur at different times due to phase shifts (V_L leads current by 90째, V_C lags by 90째). The total voltage is their vector (phasor) sum, not their scalar sum.

$$ V_m = \sqrt{V_{R,m}^2 + (V_{L,m} - V_{C,m})^2} \le V_{R,m} + V_{L,m} + V_{C,m} $$

Resonance in Series RLC Circuits

Question (Nov/Dec 2023, Q2a.i): Discuss the phenomenon of resonance in a series RLC circuit.

Discussion of Resonance

Definition: Resonance is the condition where \(X_L = X_C\), occurring at the resonant frequency \( \omega_0 = 1/\sqrt{LC} \).
Characteristics:
  • Minimum Impedance: \( Z = R \). The circuit becomes purely resistive.
  • Maximum Current: \( I_{max} = V/R \).
  • Unity Power Factor: Phase angle \( \phi = 0 \), meaning current and voltage are in phase, leading to maximum power transfer.

Phasor Relationships

Question (Nov/Dec 2023, Q2b.i): "The voltage across the capacitor lags the current by \( \pi/2 \)". Explain.

Explanation

Physical Reason: Current must flow to deposit charge on capacitor plates. Voltage only builds up after charge has accumulated. Thus, current flow precedes voltage change, so voltage lags current.
Mathematical Proof: If \( i(t) = I_m \cos(\omega t) \), then \( v_C(t) = \frac{1}{C}\int i(t) dt = \frac{I_m}{\omega C} \sin(\omega t) \). Since \( \sin(\theta) = \cos(\theta - \pi/2) \), the voltage waveform is phase-shifted by \(-\pi/2\) relative to the current.

Future Exam Preparation: Model Questions

Question 1: An inductor is connected to a variable frequency AC source with a constant peak voltage of 50 V. At 100 Hz, the peak current is 2.0 A. What will the peak current be if the frequency is increased to 400 Hz?

Model Answer 1

1. First, find the inductance L. The initial reactance is \( X_{L1} = V_m/I_{m1} = 50\text{V}/2.0\text{A} = 25 \, \Omega \).

2. Since \( X_{L1} = 2\pi f_1 L \), we have \( L = X_{L1}/(2\pi f_1) = 25 / (2\pi \times 100) \approx 0.0398 \text{ H} \).

3. Now, find the new reactance at 400 Hz. \( X_{L2} = 2\pi f_2 L = 2\pi(400)(0.0398) = 100 \, \Omega \). (Alternatively, since \(X_L \propto f\), doubling frequency twice means \(X_{L2}=4 \times X_{L1}=100\Omega\)).

4. The new peak current is \( I_{m2} = V_m/X_{L2} = 50\text{V} / 100\Omega = 0.5 \text{ A} \).

Question 2: Draw a phasor diagram for a series RLC circuit where \( X_L > X_C \). From the diagram, derive the expressions for the total impedance Z and the phase angle \( \phi \).

Model Answer 2

1. Draw the diagram: Draw the current phasor \( I_m \) along the positive x-axis. Draw \( V_{R,m} \) along the same axis. Draw \( V_{L,m} \) along the positive y-axis (leading by 90째). Draw \( V_{C,m} \) along the negative y-axis (lagging by 90째).

2. Vector Sum: The net reactive voltage is \( V_{L,m} - V_{C,m} \) along the positive y-axis. The total source voltage \( V_{s,m} \) is the hypotenuse of a right triangle with sides \( V_{R,m} \) and \( (V_{L,m} - V_{C,m}) \).

3. Derive Impedance Z: By Pythagorean theorem: \( V_{s,m}^2 = V_{R,m}^2 + (V_{L,m} - V_{C,m})^2 \). Substitute \( V=IX \): \( (I_m Z)^2 = (I_m R)^2 + (I_m X_L - I_m X_C)^2 \). Divide by \(I_m^2\) to get \( Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2} \).

4. Derive Phase Angle \( \phi \): From the triangle, \( \tan\phi = (\text{opposite})/(\text{adjacent}) = (V_{L,m} - V_{C,m}) / V_{R,m} = (X_L - X_C) / R \).
