Part 3: Applications of Electromagnetic Waves

Analysis of a Given EM Wave

Question (Jan/Feb 2023, Q1c): Given \( \vec{B} = B_0 \sin(kx - \omega t) \hat{j} \), find (i) wavelength, (ii) \( \vec{E} \), (iii) direction and magnitude of \( \vec{S} \).

Solution

(i) Wavelength: \( \lambda = 2\pi/k \).
(ii) Electric Field: The propagation direction is \(+\hat{i}\). \( \vec{S} \propto \vec{E} \times \vec{B} \) must point in \(+\hat{i}\). Since \( \vec{B} \) is \(+\hat{j}\), \( \vec{E} \) must be \(-\hat{k}\) because \( (-\hat{k}) \times (+\hat{j}) = +\hat{i} \). Amplitude is \( E_0 = c B_0 \).
$$ \vec{E} = -c B_0 \sin(kx - \omega t) \hat{k} $$
(iii) Poynting Vector: Direction is \(+\hat{i}\). Time-averaged magnitude (intensity) is:
$$ I = \langle S \rangle = \frac{c B_0^2}{2\mu_0} $$

Solar Constant and Total Power

Question (Nov/Dec 2023, Q1c): Given \( \langle S \rangle = 1.35 \times 10^3 \) W/m², (i) find \(E_m\) and \(B_m\). (ii) Find the total power radiated by the Sun at distance R = 1.5 x 10¹¹ m.

Solution

(i) E and B field magnitudes:

\( E_m = \sqrt{2\mu_0 c \langle S \rangle} = \sqrt{2 (4\pi \times 10^{-7}) (3 \times 10^8) (1.35 \times 10^3)} \approx 1009 \text{ V/m} \)

\( B_m = E_m / c \approx 3.36 \times 10^{-6} \text{ T} \)

(ii) Total Power Radiated: Total power is intensity multiplied by the surface area of a sphere of radius R. $$ P = \langle S \rangle \times (4\pi R^2) = (1.35 \times 10^3) \times (4\pi (1.5 \times 10^{11})^2) \approx 3.82 \times 10^{26} \text{ W} $$

Standing Waves

Question (Nov/Dec 2023, Q1d): Calculate the intensity of the standing wave given by \(E_y = 2E_0\cos(kx)\cos(\omega t)\) and \(B_z = 2B_0\sin(kx)\sin(\omega t)\). Prove no net energy transfer.

Solution

1. Poynting Vector \( \vec{S} \): \( \vec{S} = \frac{1}{\mu_0}(\vec{E} \times \vec{B}) = \frac{E_y B_z}{\mu_0} \hat{i} = \frac{4E_0 B_0}{\mu_0} \cos(kx)\sin(kx)\cos(\omega t)\sin(\omega t) \hat{i} \).
2. Simplify: Using \( \sin(2\alpha)=2\sin\alpha\cos\alpha \), we get \( S_x = \frac{E_0 B_0}{\mu_0} \sin(2kx) \sin(2\omega t) \).
3. Intensity (Time-Average): The average of \( \sin(2\omega t) \) over a full cycle is zero.
$$ I = \langle S_x \rangle = 0 $$
This proves there is no net energy propagation.

EMF in a Loop Antenna

Question (Jan/Feb 2023, Q1d): Find the ratio of emf from a TV wave (100 MHz) to a radio wave (1 MHz) if both have equal E-field intensities.

Solution

1. Induced EMF: From Faraday's Law, \( \mathcal{E} = -d\Phi_B/dt \). The peak EMF is \( \mathcal{E}_m = A \omega B_m \).
2. Relate to E-field: Since \( B_m = E_m/c \) and \( E_m \) is constant, \( \mathcal{E}_m = A \omega (E_m/c) \). This shows \( \mathcal{E}_m \propto \omega \propto f \).
3. Form the ratio:
$$ \frac{\mathcal{E}_{\text{TV}}}{\mathcal{E}_{\text{Radio}}} = \frac{f_{\text{TV}}}{f_{\text{Radio}}} = \frac{100 \text{ MHz}}{1 \text{ MHz}} = 100 $$

Future Exam Preparation: Model Questions

Question 1 (Nov/Dec 2023, Q3b.iii based): A 10 MHz uniform plane wave travels in a nonmagnetic (\(\mu = \mu_0\)) medium with relative permittivity \( \varepsilon_r = 9 \). Find: (a) the phase velocity, (b) the wavenumber, (c) the wavelength, and (d) the intrinsic impedance of the medium.

Model Answer 1

(a) Phase Velocity (v): The refractive index is \( n = \sqrt{\varepsilon_r \mu_r} = \sqrt{9 \times 1} = 3 \). The velocity is \( v = c/n = (3 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}) / 3 = 1 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s} \).

(b) Wavenumber (k): The angular frequency is \( \omega = 2\pi f = 2\pi(10 \times 10^6) \text{ rad/s} \). The wavenumber is \( k = \omega/v = 2\pi \times 10^7 / (1 \times 10^8) = 0.2\pi \approx 0.628 \text{ rad/m} \).

(c) Wavelength (\(\lambda\)): \( \lambda = 2\pi/k = 2\pi / (0.2\pi) = 10 \text{ m} \).

(d) Intrinsic Impedance (\(\eta\)): \( \eta = \sqrt{\mu/\varepsilon} = \sqrt{\mu_0/(\varepsilon_r \varepsilon_0)} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\varepsilon_r}}\sqrt{\mu_0/\varepsilon_0} \). The term \( \sqrt{\mu_0/\varepsilon_0} \) is the impedance of free space, \( \eta_0 \approx 377 \, \Omega \). So, \( \eta = \eta_0 / \sqrt{\varepsilon_r} = 377 / 3 \approx 125.7 \, \Omega \).
