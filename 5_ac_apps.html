Designing an LC Circuit

Question (Jan/Feb 2023, Q2a.iii): It is desired to set up an un-driven LC circuit in which the capacitor is originally charged to a potential difference of 100 V. The maximum current is to be 10 A, and the oscillation frequency is to be 1000 Hz. What are the required values of L and C?

Solution

Step 1: Use Conservation of Energy. The maximum energy stored in the capacitor (\(E_C\)) must equal the maximum energy stored in the inductor (\(E_L\)). $$ E_{C,max} = E_{L,max} \quad \implies \quad \frac{1}{2} C V_m^2 = \frac{1}{2} L I_m^2 $$ $$ C V_m^2 = L I_m^2 $$
Step 2: Use the oscillation frequency formula. The natural angular frequency is \( \omega_0 = 2\pi f = 1/\sqrt{LC} \). $$ \omega_0^2 = \frac{1}{LC} \quad \implies \quad L = \frac{1}{\omega_0^2 C} $$
Step 3: Substitute L from Step 2 into Step 1 to solve for C. $$ C V_m^2 = \left( \frac{1}{\omega_0^2 C} \right) I_m^2 \quad \implies \quad C^2 = \frac{I_m^2}{V_m^2 \omega_0^2} $$ $$ C = \frac{I_m}{V_m \omega_0} = \frac{I_m}{V_m (2\pi f)} $$ $$ C = \frac{10 \text{ A}}{(100 \text{ V})(2\pi \times 1000 \text{ Hz})} = \frac{1}{20000\pi} \approx 1.59 \times 10^{-5} \text{ F} $$
Step 4: Use the value of C to find L. $$ L = \frac{1}{\omega_0^2 C} = \frac{1}{(2\pi f)^2 C} = \frac{1}{(2\pi \times 1000)^2 (1.59 \times 10^{-5})} \approx 1.59 \times 10^{-3} \text{ H} $$
$$ C \approx 15.9 \text{ ÂµF} \quad \text{and} \quad L \approx 1.59 \text{ mH} $$

Quality Factor (Q) and Voltage Magnification

Question (Jan/Feb 2023, Q2b): (i) What is it meant by the Quality factor (Q) of a circuit? (ii) Prove that the voltage magnification of a RLC circuit is equal to its Q at resonance.

Solution

(i) Meaning of Quality Factor (Q): The Quality factor (Q) is a dimensionless parameter that describes the sharpness of the resonance peak in a resonant circuit. A high Q-factor implies a narrow bandwidth and a highly selective circuit (responds strongly only to frequencies very near resonance). It can be defined as the ratio of the resonant frequency to the bandwidth, \( Q = \omega_0 / \Delta\omega \).
(ii) Proof of Voltage Magnification: Voltage magnification refers to the fact that at resonance, the peak voltage across the inductor (\(V_L\)) or capacitor (\(V_C\)) can be many times larger than the peak source voltage (\(V_s\)).

Step A: At resonance, the current is maximum: \( I_m = V_{s,m} / R \).

Step B: The peak voltage across the inductor is \( V_{L,m} = I_m X_L = I_m (\omega_0 L) \).

Step C: Substitute the expression for \(I_m\) from Step A into Step B.

$$ V_{L,m} = \left( \frac{V_{s,m}}{R} \right) (\omega_0 L) = V_{s,m} \left( \frac{\omega_0 L}{R} \right) $$

Step D: The term \( (\omega_0 L / R) \) is the definition of the Quality Factor, Q.

$$ V_{L,m} = Q \cdot V_{s,m} \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{V_{L,m}}{V_{s,m}} = Q $$
This proves that the ratio of the peak inductor voltage to the peak source voltage (the voltage magnification) is equal to Q. A similar proof holds for the capacitor.

Capacitor Behavior with Changing Frequency

Question (Nov/Dec 2023, Q2b.ii): When a particular capacitor is connected to a sinusoidal voltage with 110 V amplitude, a peak current of 3 A appears. Calculate the peak current and the capacitive reactance if the frequency of the applied voltage is doubled.

Solution

Step 1: Find the initial capacitive reactance (\(X_{C1}\)). $$ X_{C1} = \frac{V_m}{I_{m1}} = \frac{110 \text{ V}}{3 \text{ A}} \approx 36.67 \, \Omega $$
Step 2: Find the new capacitive reactance (\(X_{C2}\)) when frequency is doubled.

Capacitive reactance is inversely proportional to frequency: \( X_C = 1 / (2\pi f C) \).

If \( f_2 = 2f_1 \), then \( X_{C2} = \frac{1}{2\pi (2f_1) C} = \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{1}{2\pi f_1 C} \right) = \frac{1}{2} X_{C1} \).

$$ X_{C2} = \frac{36.67 \, \Omega}{2} \approx 18.33 \, \Omega $$
Step 3: Find the new peak current (\(I_{m2}\)).

The voltage amplitude remains 110 V.

$$ I_{m2} = \frac{V_m}{X_{C2}} = \frac{110 \text{ V}}{18.33 \, \Omega} = 6 \text{ A} $$
New peak current = 6 A. New capacitive reactance = 18.33 \(\Omega\).

Average Power in an RLC Circuit

Question (Nov/Dec 2023, Q2a.ii): Show that the average power delivered to a series RLC circuit is given by \( P_{av} = \frac{V_{rms}^2 R \omega^2}{R^2 \omega^2 + L^2(\omega_0^2 - \omega^2)^2} \). Also show the peak current and phase angle expressions.

Solution

Part A: Deriving Average Power

Step 1: Start with the standard formula for average power: \( P_{av} = I_{rms}^2 R \).

Step 2: Express \(I_{rms}\) in terms of \(V_{rms}\) and impedance Z: \( I_{rms} = V_{rms} / Z \).

$$ P_{av} = \left(\frac{V_{rms}}{Z}\right)^2 R = \frac{V_{rms}^2 R}{Z^2} $$

Step 3: Write out the expression for \(Z^2\).

$$ Z^2 = R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2 = R^2 + \left(\omega L - \frac{1}{\omega C}\right)^2 $$

Step 4: Substitute \( C = 1/(\omega_0^2 L) \) from the resonance condition.

$$ Z^2 = R^2 + \left(\omega L - \frac{\omega_0^2 L}{\omega}\right)^2 = R^2 + L^2 \left(\omega - \frac{\omega_0^2}{\omega}\right)^2 = R^2 + L^2 \left(\frac{\omega^2 - \omega_0^2}{\omega}\right)^2 $$ $$ Z^2 = R^2 + \frac{L^2(\omega^2 - \omega_0^2)^2}{\omega^2} $$

Step 5: Substitute this \(Z^2\) into the power equation and rearrange.

$$ P_{av} = \frac{V_{rms}^2 R}{R^2 + \frac{L^2(\omega^2 - \omega_0^2)^2}{\omega^2}} = \frac{V_{rms}^2 R \omega^2}{R^2 \omega^2 + L^2(\omega^2 - \omega_0^2)^2} $$
Part B: Deriving Peak Current and Phase Angle

Peak Current (\(I_m\)): Using the expression for Z from Step 4:

$$ I_m = \frac{V_m}{Z} = \frac{V_m}{\sqrt{R^2 + \frac{L^2(\omega_0^2 - \omega^2)^2}{\omega^2}}} = \frac{V_m \omega}{\sqrt{R^2\omega^2 + L^2(\omega_0^2 - \omega^2)^2}} $$ This matches the form given in the question: \( I_m = \omega V_m [(\omega R)^2 + L^2(\omega_0^2 - \omega^2)^2]^{-1/2} \).

Phase Angle (\(\phi\)):

$$ \tan\phi = \frac{X_L - X_C}{R} = \frac{\omega L - 1/(\omega C)}{R} = \frac{L}{R}\left(\omega - \frac{1}{\omega LC}\right) $$ Substitute \( 1/LC = \omega_0^2 \): $$ \tan\phi = \frac{L}{R}\left(\omega - \frac{\omega_0^2}{\omega}\right) = \frac{L(\omega^2 - \omega_0^2)}{R\omega} $$ This matches the form required.
